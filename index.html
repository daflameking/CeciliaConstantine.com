About

Computer Science student by day and developer by night, based in sunny South Florida. I've always been interested in helping the world "with my own two hands" and so, I originally started my career with a Biological Science degree from Florida Atlantic University and started my career at one of the largest blood plasma laboratory companies in the US. I also volunteered with the US Naval Sea Cadet Corps, providing mentorship to my local Enterprise Division.

While volunteering to assist other departments and taking up data entry duties I realized that the programs and technology behind lab assays and turnaround times, often a critical component in deliverables, involved software. A simple application swap could change the entire process and volume move.

I started programming in 2016 while attending a government funded vocational program nightly for a couple months. Shortly afterward, I received my MTA certification, acceptance into Western Governors University Software Development degree program, and offer to intern at a local FAU-backed startup backed full-time! Read more about my work background on LinkedIn.

During that year long position, I wore several hats that allowed me to build websites, create open-source projects for museums, win a Hackathon, and even use A+ knowledge to troubleshoot hardware.

Now, I'm enrolled full-time as a Computer Science student and freelance as a software developer for local businesses. I love the idea of coding for a cause, for a difference. Constantly learning, I'm interested in growing my skillset and giving back to the community.
